1

Executive Summary

A three-stage discrete Class AB audio power amplifier was derived analytically from a 25 W into 4 Ω target spec at ±22 V supply rails — not copied from a reference design — then verified in LTspice, laid out and fabricated as a PCB, hand-soldered, and bench-tested against a live 4 Ω speaker load. Measured output power came within 0.72% of the 25 W target.

2

Problem Statement

Design and physically build an audio power amplifier meeting a specific power/load/supply target while avoiding crossover distortion inherent to naive Class B output stages — and prove the result with measured hardware data, not just a simulation.

System Architecture

Three stages: a differential input pair (2N5401/2N5551) providing error sensing and feedback summing; a bootstrapped voltage-amplification stage (VAS) providing the bulk of open-loop gain; and an improved push-pull Class AB output stage (TIP3055/TIP2955 power devices driven through TIP41C/TIP42 Darlington drivers). Every stage's component values were derived analytically from the 25 W / 4 Ω / ±22 V spec.

6

Implementation

  • Hand-derived every stage: supply headroom from required peak voltage/current, emitter-degeneration resistor sizing and power dissipation, Vbe-multiplier bias generation for crossover-distortion-free Class AB operation, and passive filter corner frequencies (input high-pass, feedback DC-block).
  • Fabricated the PCB via UV photolithography and ferric-chloride etching.
  • Hand-soldered all components and bench-tested with a live 4 Ω speaker load.
7

Testing

Verified first in LTspice against the analytical targets, then re-measured on the physical board under a live 4 Ω resistive/speaker load, including collector-current waveform inspection to confirm true Class AB conduction.

9

Engineering Decisions

Chose a Vbe-multiplier bias network over fixed-diode biasing to allow fine adjustment of quiescent current post-fabrication — necessary because component tolerances on hand-soldered discrete boards shift the ideal bias point from the simulated value.

10

Lessons Learned

Deriving every stage analytically from the spec — rather than adapting a published reference topology — made it possible to explain and defend every component value, and made the sub-1% measured error against simulation achievable because every assumption was traceable back to a first-principles equation.

11

Future Improvements

  • Add short-circuit / thermal protection circuitry for unattended operation.
  • Replace the linear supply with a switching supply to reduce heatsink mass.
  • Characterize THD+N across the full audio band with an audio analyzer.
